## 1. Mathematical Foundation

The geometry is constructed on a parametric cylindrical helix $\mathbf{x}^s(s)$. To transform this line into a 3D surface (tube), we attach a circle of radius $r(s)$ at each point of the curve, oriented in the normal plane of **Frenet-Serret**.

### 1.1. The Central Helix

The central curve is defined by:
$$
\mathbf{x}^s(s) = \begin{pmatrix} R \cos(\omega s) \\ R \sin(\omega s) \\ k s \end{pmatrix}
$$
Where:
- **$R$:** Radius of the helix.
- **$\omega$:** Angular velocity ($2\pi \cdot \text{turns} / \text{height}$).
- **$k$:** Vertical inclination factor (pitch).

### 1.2. The Frenet Frame

To orient the cross-section of the tube, we calculate the local orthonormal basis:
1. **Normal ($\vec{N}$):** Points towards the central axis of the helix.
   $$\vec{N} = (-\cos(\omega s), -\sin(\omega s), 0)$$
2. **Tangent ($\vec{T}$):** Normalized derivative of $\mathbf{x}^s(s)$.
3. **Binormal ($\vec{B}$):** Defines the plane of the circle.
   $$\vec{B} = \vec{T} \times \vec{N}$$

---

## 2. Surface Construction (Variable Radius)

The surface $\mathbf{C}(s,u)$ is generated by adding a radial component in the plane $(\vec{N}, \vec{B})$:

$$
\mathbf{C}(s,u) = \mathbf{x}^s(s) + r(s) \bigl [ \vec{N} \cos(u) + \vec{B} \sin(u) \bigr ]
$$

### 2.1. Linear Variation of the Radius

We implement a linear transition from the base radius ($r_{base}$) to the tip radius ($r_{tip}$):

$$
r(s) = r_{base} + (r_{tip} - r_{base}) \cdot \frac{s}{s_{max}}
$$

## 3. Numerical Implementation

### Discretization
- **$N_s$:** Points along the length (s-axis).
- **$N_u$:** Points around the circular section (u-axis).

### Mesh Connectivity (Triangulation)
For a grid of vertices $V_{i,j}$, each quadrilateral cell is divided into two triangles:
1. **Triangle A:** $(V_{i,j}, V_{i+1,j}, V_{i,j+1})$
2. **Triangle B:** $(V_{i+1,j}, V_{i+1,j+1}, V_{i,j+1})$
